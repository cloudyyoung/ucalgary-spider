Junior Course
 
Gender and Sexuality Studies 201       Talking Gender and Sexuality
An interdisciplinary and intersectional examination of the social and cultural constructions of gender and sexuality. Uses a series of case studies involving historical events and current affairs to discuss how ideas about gender and sexual identity shape our social systems, cultural expression, and political values. The Canadian context is emphasized.
Course Hours: 3 units; (3-0)
Also known as: (formerly Women’s Studies 201)

Gender and Sexuality Studies 311       Theorizing Gender and Sexuality
Explores contemporary approaches to theorizing gender and sexuality, with attention to their historical bases. Includes consideration of feminist, queer, trans, and intersectional theories.

Gender and Sexuality Studies 501       Independent Research
Supervised individual study of a special topic. Intended primarily for students in Gender and Sexuality Studies programs.
Course Hours: 3 units; (12-36 hours)
Prerequisite(s): Consent of the Department.
Notes: Students wishing to register in this class must first consult with the Department, and are responsible for proposing a topic and securing a supervisor.
Also known as: (formerly Women’s Studies 501)
MAY BE REPEATED FOR CREDIT
back to top
Gender and Sexuality Studies 503       Community Service Learning
An opportunity for students to learn from volunteer service in community organizing and/or activism relating to feminist, gender, and sexuality studies. Students will present findings related to their community engagement placement to the class in scheduled sessions during the term.
Course Hours: 3 units; (1.5S-2)
Prerequisite(s): Women's Studies 201, 405 or Gender and Sexuality Studies 201, 405 and consent of the Department.
Notes: Students wishing to register in the class must first secure a volunteer position and have that position approved by the department. Volunteer commitment over the term must be between 20-25 hours.
Also known as: (formerly Women’s Studies 503)
back to top
Gender and Sexuality Studies 590       Honours Thesis

Prerequisite(s): Admission to the Honours program in Gender and Sexuality Studies and consent of the Department.
Notes: Students are responsible for securing a thesis supervisor prior to enrolling in the class.
